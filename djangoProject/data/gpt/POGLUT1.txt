Human POGLUT1 (Protein O-Glucosyltransferase 1), also known as Rumi, is an enzyme crucial for the post-translational modification of certain proteins through O-glucosylation. POGLUT1 specifically adds O-glucose to serine or threonine residues in the epidermal growth factor-like (EGF-like) repeats found in a variety of proteins, including the Notch receptors. This modification is essential for the proper folding and functioning of these proteins.

POGLUT1's activity is particularly significant in the context of the Notch signaling pathway, a critical pathway in cell fate determination, differentiation, and development. The proper functioning of the Notch signaling pathway is essential for normal embryonic development and the maintenance of adult tissues. POGLUT1-mediated O-glucosylation of EGF-like repeats in Notch receptors is crucial for their activity and stability on the cell surface.

Located in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), POGLUT1 is involved in the early stages of the protein modification process. Mutations in the POGLUT1 gene can lead to disorders in Notch signaling, resulting in developmental abnormalities and diseases.

A notable condition associated with POGLUT1 dysfunction is Dowling-Degos disease, a genetic skin disorder characterized by hyperpigmentation. Additionally, altered Notch signaling due to POGLUT1 mutations has been implicated in various types of cancer and other developmental disorders.
