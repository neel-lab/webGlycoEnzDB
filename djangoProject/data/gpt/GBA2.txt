Human GBA2 (glucocerebrosidase 2) is an enzyme with a critical role in lipid metabolism, distinct from GBA1.

Function
GBA2 is involved in the breakdown of glucosylceramide into glucose and ceramide. Unlike GBA1, which is lysosomal, GBA2 is non-lysosomal and has a different substrate specificity. GBA2's activity is vital for the metabolism of complex lipids and maintenance of cellular lipid homeostasis.

Reaction Pathways
GBA2 is part of the glucosylceramide metabolism pathway but operates in different cellular compartments compared to GBA1. It catalyzes the hydrolysis of glucosylceramide, a critical step in the catabolism of glycosphingolipids. These lipids are important components of cell membranes and are involved in various cellular processes including signaling.

Location
GBA2 is predominantly found in the endoplasmic reticulum and possibly other non-lysosomal cellular compartments. Its location is crucial for its role in lipid metabolism as it ensures the breakdown of glucosylceramide in areas where lysosomal enzymes like GBA1 are not present.

Related Diseases

Given its role in glucosylceramide catabolism, aberrations in GBA2 may contribute to lipid storage diseases or other metabolic syndromes.
