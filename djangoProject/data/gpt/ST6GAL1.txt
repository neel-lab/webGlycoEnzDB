### Function
Human ST6GAL1, or beta-galactoside alpha-2,6-sialyltransferase 1, is an enzyme that catalyzes the addition of sialic acid to glycoproteins and glycolipids, specifically in an alpha-2,6 linkage to the terminal galactose residues on N-glycans and O-glycans. This sialylation process is crucial for the regulation of various biological functions, including cell-cell communication, immune response modulation, and the stability of glycoproteins.

This process results in the formation of alpha-2,6-sialylated glycoproteins and glycolipids, which are involved in multiple cellular processes. The enzyme's activity is essential for the synthesis of sialylated structures that regulate the binding of cells to extracellular matrix components, modulate receptor signaling, and influence the lifespan of circulating glycoproteins.

ST6GAL1 is primarily localized in the Golgi apparatus, the key cellular organelle responsible for the post-translational modification of proteins and lipids.
