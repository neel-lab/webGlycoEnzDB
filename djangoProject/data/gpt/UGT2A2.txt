### Function
Human UGT2A2, or UDP-glucuronosyltransferase 2A2, is an enzyme involved in the metabolism and detoxification of various endogenous and exogenous compounds. Similar to other UGT family members, UGT2A2 catalyzes the glucuronidation process, where it transfers glucuronic acid to lipophilic substances, making them more water-soluble and thus easier to excrete from the body. UGT2A2 is particularly important in the metabolism of odorants, steroids, and certain environmental toxins.

UGT2A2 functions within the glucuronidation pathway, a crucial phase II metabolic process that enhances the excretion of hydrophobic molecules. This reaction increases the solubility of these substances, facilitating their elimination through urine or bile, and reducing their potential toxicity.

UGT2A2 is primarily expressed in the nasal epithelium, where it plays a key role in the detoxification of inhaled substances, including odorants and environmental pollutants. This localization allows UGT2A2 to act as a first line of defense in the respiratory tract, processing volatile compounds before they can enter systemic circulation. UGT2A2 is also found in other tissues, including the liver, though at lower levels, where it contributes to the broader metabolism of steroids and other compounds.
