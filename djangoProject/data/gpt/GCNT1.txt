Human GCNT1, also known as Beta-1,3-Galactosyl-O-glycosyl-glycoprotein Beta-1,6-N-acetylglucosaminyltransferase 1, is an enzyme that plays a crucial role in the biosynthesis of complex O-glycans.

Function
GCNT1 is responsible for adding N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) in a beta-1,6 linkage to a core 2 O-glycan structure. This enzymatic action is fundamental for the formation of branched O-glycans, which are important for various cellular functions, including cell signaling, adhesion, and immune response. The activity of GCNT1 in synthesizing branched O-glycans affects the glycosylation pattern of proteins and thus impacts their function and localization.

Reaction Pathways
GCNT1 participates in the pathway of O-glycan biosynthesis. Its specific role involves the addition of GlcNAc to the core 2 branch of O-glycans, which can lead to further elongation and modification of these glycan structures. The core 2 O-glycans synthesized by GCNT1 are involved in various biological processes and can be found on many glycoproteins, especially those on the cell surface.

Location
The structure of GCNT1 suggests a type II membrane topology, indicating its localization in the Golgi apparatus. The Golgi apparatus is the cellular site where glycosylation, including the addition of sugars to proteins and lipids, takes place. The enzyme’s structure includes an N-terminal cytoplasmic domain, a transmembrane segment, and a stem region and catalytic domain​​.
