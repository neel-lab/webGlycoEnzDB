### Function
Human HEXA, or beta-hexosaminidase A, is an essential lysosomal enzyme that plays a critical role in the degradation of GM2 gangliosides, which are complex molecules found in nerve cell membranes. HEXA catalyzes the hydrolysis of the terminal N-acetylgalactosamine residue from GM2 ganglioside, converting it into GM3 ganglioside. This reaction is crucial for the proper breakdown and recycling of gangliosides, preventing their accumulation in cells.

### Diseases
Mutations in the HEXA gene cause Tay-Sachs disease, a severe lysosomal storage disorder characterized by the accumulation of GM2 gangliosides in neurons. This accumulation occurs because of a deficiency or malfunction of HEXA, leading to progressive neurodegeneration, developmental delays, and early death. Tay-Sachs disease primarily affects infants, with symptoms appearing within the first few months of life. There is currently no cure for Tay-Sachs, and treatment is focused on managing symptoms and improving quality of life.
