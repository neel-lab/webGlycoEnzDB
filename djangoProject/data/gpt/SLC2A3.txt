### Function
Human SLC2A3, also known as GLUT3, is a high-affinity glucose transporter that plays a crucial role in providing glucose to cells with high energy demands, particularly in the brain. GLUT3 is responsible for facilitating the uptake of glucose from the bloodstream into cells, ensuring that these cells have a continuous supply of glucose, which is essential for ATP production and normal cellular function, especially in neurons.

### Reaction Pathways
GLUT3 functions within the glucose transport pathway, where it mediates the facilitated diffusion of glucose across cell membranes. Due to its high affinity for glucose, GLUT3 is highly efficient at transporting glucose even when extracellular glucose concentrations are low. This property makes GLUT3 particularly important in tissues like the brain, where glucose is the primary energy source, and consistent glucose supply is critical for maintaining cognitive functions and overall brain health.

### Location
SLC2A3 is primarily expressed in tissues with high glucose demands, including the brain, placenta, and neurons. In the brain, GLUT3 is the predominant glucose transporter in neurons, where it ensures an adequate glucose supply for synaptic activity and neurotransmitter synthesis. Its expression in the placenta also supports fetal development by facilitating glucose transport to the growing fetus.

### Diseases
Dysfunction or altered expression of GLUT3 can lead to severe consequences, particularly in the central nervous system, where glucose availability is tightly linked to neuronal function. While specific genetic disorders directly related to SLC2A3 mutations are not well-documented, impaired glucose transport due to altered GLUT3 function may contribute to neurological disorders, cognitive deficits, and conditions like ischemia, where reduced blood flow to the brain limits glucose supply. Understanding the role of GLUT3 in glucose transport is essential for exploring potential therapeutic strategies for neurological diseases linked to energy metabolism.
