### Function
Human UGT3A1, or UDP-glucuronosyltransferase 3A1, is an enzyme involved in the conjugation and detoxification of various endogenous and exogenous compounds through glucuronidation and glycosidation processes. Unlike other UGT enzymes, UGT3A1 does not use glucuronic acid as a donor molecule. Instead, it transfers glucose or N-acetylglucosamine to its substrates. This enzyme is essential for modifying and inactivating a variety of compounds, including some hormones, environmental toxins, and drugs, to make them more water-soluble and easier for the body to eliminate.

UGT3A1 is involved in the metabolism of a diverse range of compounds, including bile acids and potentially toxic xenobiotics.
