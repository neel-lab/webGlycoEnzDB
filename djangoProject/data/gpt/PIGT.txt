Human PIGT (Phosphatidylinositol Glycan Anchor Biosynthesis Class T) is a crucial enzyme in the biosynthesis of glycosylphosphatidylinositol (GPI) anchors, which are complex glycolipids that anchor various proteins to the cell membrane. PIGT plays a significant role in the later stages of GPI anchor assembly, particularly in the attachment of the GPI anchor to proteins.

PIGT is primarily located in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), where it collaborates with other enzymes to complete the GPI anchor biosynthesis and facilitate the transfer of the anchor to specific proteins. This process is essential for the correct localization and function of GPI-anchored proteins on the cell surface. GPI-anchored proteins, including enzymes, receptors, and adhesion molecules, are involved in numerous biological processes such as signal transduction, cell adhesion, and immune responses.

Mutations or dysfunction in PIGT can lead to GPI anchor deficiencies, resulting in a group of rare genetic disorders known as GPI biosynthesis defects (GPIBDs). Patients with GPIBDs may exhibit a range of symptoms, including developmental delays, intellectual disabilities, seizures, and various neurological and physical abnormalities. This underscores the critical role of GPI anchors in normal development and cellular functioning.
