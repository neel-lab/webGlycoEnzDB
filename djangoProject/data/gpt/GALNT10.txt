### Function
Human GALNT10, or polypeptide N-acetylgalactosaminyltransferase 10, is an enzyme involved in the O-linked glycosylation process. It catalyzes the transfer of N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc) from UDP-GalNAc to the serine or threonine residues of target proteins, forming the core O-GalNAc glycan structure. This glycosylation is essential for protein stability, function, and interactions within the cell, particularly in processes such as cell signaling and communication.

### Reaction Pathways
GALNT10 plays a critical role in the O-glycosylation pathway by initiating the formation of mucin-type O-GalNAc glycans. It specifically transfers GalNAc to serine or threonine residues on proteins, which is the first step in the complex process of glycan chain elongation and branching. This initial glycosylation event by GALNT10 allows for subsequent modifications by other glycosyltransferases, leading to the creation of diverse glycan structures that contribute to the function and regulation of various glycoproteins.

GALNT10 is predominantly localized in the Golgi apparatus, the organelle responsible for the post-translational modification of proteins. Within the Golgi, GALNT10 facilitates the glycosylation of proteins as they are processed and sorted for transport to different parts of the cell or for secretion. The enzyme's localization in the Golgi is crucial for its role in ensuring that proteins are correctly glycosylated before they reach their final destinations.
