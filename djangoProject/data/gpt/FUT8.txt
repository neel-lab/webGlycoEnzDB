### Function

Human FUT8 (fucosyltransferase 8) is an enzyme responsible for catalyzing the transfer of a fucose residue from GDP-fucose to the innermost N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) of N-glycans in an α1,6 linkage. This process is known as core fucosylation and is critical for the proper function of glycoproteins. Core fucosylation by FUT8 affects various biological processes, including cell signaling, immune responses, and the stability of glycoproteins.

### Reaction Pathways

FUT8 operates within the N-glycan biosynthesis pathway, where it plays a pivotal role in the core fucosylation of glycoproteins. The enzyme transfers fucose to the GlcNAc residue on the asparagine-linked N-glycan core, a modification essential for the proper folding, stability, and function of glycoproteins. This modification is particularly important in the maturation and activity of growth factor receptors and immune molecules.

### Location

FUT8 is localized in the Golgi apparatus, where it performs its glycosylation functions. The Golgi is the central site for the modification and processing of proteins and lipids in the cell. FUT8’s presence in the Golgi ensures that glycoproteins are correctly fucosylated before they are transported to their final destinations, such as the cell membrane or extracellular space.

### Diseases

Mutations or dysregulation of FUT8 have been linked to several diseases, most notably chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and various cancers. Studies have shown that reduced FUT8 activity is associated with a faster decline in lung function and increased severity in COPD patients. Additionally, overexpression of FUT8 has been observed in certain cancers, where it contributes to tumor progression and metastasis by altering cell adhesion and signaling.

In summary, FUT8 is a critical enzyme involved in core fucosylation within the Golgi apparatus. Its role is vital in maintaining the structure and function of glycoproteins, with significant implications in diseases like COPD and cancer.
