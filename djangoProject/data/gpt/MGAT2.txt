### Function
Human MGAT2, or N-acetylglucosaminyltransferase II, is an essential enzyme in the biosynthesis of N-linked glycoproteins. MGAT2 catalyzes the addition of N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) to the core mannose residues of glycoproteins, a critical step in forming complex and hybrid N-glycans. These glycan structures are crucial for the stability, folding, and function of glycoproteins, which are involved in various cellular processes, including cell signaling, immune responses, and protein trafficking.

### Reaction Pathways
MGAT2 operates within the N-glycosylation pathway, a critical post-translational modification process for proteins. Specifically, MGAT2 acts in the medial-Golgi apparatus, where it transfers a GlcNAc residue to the alpha-1,6-linked mannose of the core oligosaccharide structure attached to glycoproteins. This enzymatic action is necessary for branching N-glycans, leading to the formation of complex and hybrid glycan structures. These N-glycans are essential for determining the biological activity, stability, and cell surface expression of glycoproteins.

### Location
MGAT2 is localized in the Golgi apparatus, particularly in the medial-Golgi compartment. The Golgi apparatus is responsible for modifying, sorting, and packaging proteins and lipids before they are transported to their final destinations within the cell or secreted outside the cell. The localization of MGAT2 within the Golgi is crucial for its role in the later stages of N-glycan processing, ensuring that glycoproteins are correctly modified and functional.

### Diseases
Mutations or deficiencies in the MGAT2 gene are linked to congenital disorders of glycosylation type IIa (CDG-IIa), a rare inherited condition characterized by severe developmental and neurological issues. CDG-IIa results from improper glycosylation of proteins due to the lack of MGAT2 activity, leading to a wide range of symptoms, including developmental delays, growth retardation, and immune system dysfunction. The disease highlights the critical role of MGAT2 in human development and cellular function.
