### Function
Human UGT2B15, or UDP-glucuronosyltransferase 2B15, is an enzyme involved in the metabolism and detoxification of various endogenous and exogenous compounds through the process of glucuronidation. UGT2B15 catalyzes the conjugation of glucuronic acid to lipophilic substances, such as steroid hormones (particularly androgens and estrogens), drugs, and environmental toxins, making them more water-soluble and easier to excrete from the body. This enzyme plays a crucial role in maintaining hormone balance and in the detoxification of drugs.

UGT2B15 functions within the glucuronidation pathway, a critical phase II metabolic process that enhances the excretion of hydrophobic compounds. UGT2B15 is also involved in the metabolism of various pharmaceuticals, including certain antiepileptic drugs and analgesics, as well as the detoxification of carcinogens. This activity is essential for regulating hormone levels and reducing the potential toxicity of drugs and environmental chemicals.

UGT2B15 is primarily expressed in the liver, the main organ responsible for drug metabolism and detoxification.
