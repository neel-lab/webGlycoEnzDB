### Function
Human SLC35A3 is a nucleotide sugar transporter that is essential for the transport of UDP-N-acetylglucosamine (UDP-GlcNAc) from the cytosol into the Golgi apparatus. This enzyme plays a crucial role in the glycosylation process, where UDP-GlcNAc is used as a donor substrate for the addition of N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) to glycoproteins and glycolipids. This modification is vital for the proper structure and function of these molecules, affecting processes such as protein folding, stability, and cell-cell communication.

### Reaction Pathways
SLC35A3 functions in the N-glycosylation pathway, specifically facilitating the transport of UDP-GlcNAc into the Golgi lumen. Once inside the Golgi, UDP-GlcNAc is utilized by glycosyltransferases to add GlcNAc residues to nascent glycoproteins and glycolipids. This process is crucial for the proper assembly of glycan structures that are critical for the function of cell surface proteins and lipids, influencing cell signaling, adhesion, and immune recognition.

SLC35A3 is localized in the Golgi apparatus, the central organelle involved in protein and lipid modification and sorting. The enzyme is embedded in the Golgi membrane, where it performs its function of transporting UDP-GlcNAc from the cytosol into the Golgi lumen. This localization is essential for ensuring that sufficient UDP-GlcNAc is available for glycosylation processes, which are crucial for the proper function of many cellular proteins and lipids.

However, disruption of SLC35A3 function can affect the glycosylation process, potentially leading to improper protein folding and function, contributing to congenital disorders of glycosylation (CDGs).
