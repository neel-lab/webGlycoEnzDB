Human NDST1 (N-Deacetylase/N-Sulfotransferase 1) is a multifunctional enzyme crucial in the biosynthesis of heparan sulfate (HS), a complex glycosaminoglycan (GAG) involved in various biological processes. NDST1 specifically catalyzes two key reactions in HS biosynthesis: the deacetylation of N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) residues and the subsequent sulfation of the resulting glucosamine residues. This dual enzymatic activity is fundamental for the formation of the sulfated domains within HS chains, which are essential for their interaction with a variety of proteins.

Located in the Golgi apparatus, NDST1 plays a pivotal role in determining the fine structure and sulfation pattern of HS. This pattern is crucial for HS's diverse functions, including cell signaling, cell adhesion, blood coagulation, and regulation of growth factor activity. HS modified by NDST1 is involved in numerous physiological and pathological processes, including developmental biology, angiogenesis, and tumor metastasis.

Mutations or dysfunctions in NDST1 can lead to alterations in HS structure and function, potentially contributing to various diseases. While specific diseases directly caused by NDST1 mutations are not well-documented, abnormal HS biosynthesis and structure have been implicated in several disorders, including cancer progression and developmental abnormalities. NDST1's role in HS biosynthesis also suggests potential implications in congenital disorders of glycosylation and other metabolic disorders involving GAGs.
