### Function

Human FUT6 (fucosyltransferase 6) is an enzyme involved in the synthesis of the Lewis antigens, specifically the Lewis X (Lex) and sialyl-Lewis X (sLex) epitopes. FUT6 catalyzes the transfer of fucose from GDP-fucose to N-acetylglucosamine residues in an α1,3 linkage, a crucial step in the formation of these glycan structures. These fucosylated glycans are important for various biological processes, including cell-cell recognition, immune response, and leukocyte adhesion during inflammation.

### Reaction Pathways

FUT6 functions within the fucosylation pathway, particularly in the synthesis of Lewis antigens. It transfers fucose in an α1,3 linkage to GlcNAc residues on glycoproteins and glycolipids, forming Lex structures. These structures can be further modified by sialyltransferases to produce sLex, a key ligand for selectins. This process is essential for mediating leukocyte rolling and adhesion to endothelial cells during immune responses, playing a vital role in inflammation and immune surveillance.

### Location

FUT6 is primarily localized in the Golgi apparatus, where it carries out its enzymatic function in the glycosylation pathway. The Golgi is the central hub for protein and lipid modification, providing the optimal environment for FUT6 to interact with other glycosyltransferases and complete the fucosylation of glycoproteins and glycolipids. The localization of FUT6 in the Golgi is critical for ensuring that the synthesized glycan structures are correctly processed and functional when they reach the cell surface.

### Diseases

Mutations or altered expression of FUT6 can impact the synthesis of Lewis antigens, potentially leading to disorders associated with abnormal cell adhesion and immune responses. Changes in FUT6 activity have been observed in various diseases, including cancer, where altered fucosylation patterns can contribute to tumor progression and metastasis. Additionally, individuals with certain FUT6 polymorphisms may have differences in their susceptibility to infections and inflammatory conditions.
