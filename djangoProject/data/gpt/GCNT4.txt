Human GCNT4, also known as Beta-1,6-N-acetylglucosaminyltransferase 4, is an enzyme that plays a critical role in the biosynthesis of complex glycoconjugates.

Function
GCNT4 is involved in the addition of N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) residues to glycoconjugates, specifically in a beta-1,6 linkage. This enzymatic activity is essential for the formation of branched glycan structures, which are important components of cell membranes and play vital roles in cell signaling, adhesion, and immune response. The action of GCNT4 influences the glycosylation pattern of proteins, affecting their function and localization.

Reaction Pathways
GCNT4 participates in the pathway of glycoprotein biosynthesis, particularly in the elaboration of branched O-glycans and possibly other glycan structures. Its role involves the addition of GlcNAc to specific glycan substrates, leading to the formation of complex glycan structures found on many cell surface proteins and involved in various biological processes.

Location
GCNT4, like many glycosyltransferases, is a type II membrane protein, suggesting its localization in the Golgi apparatus. The Golgi apparatus is the cellular site where glycosylation, the addition of sugars to proteins or lipids, takes place. The structure of GCNT4, including a C-terminal catalytic domain, a stem region, a transmembrane domain, and an N-terminal cytoplasmic domain, is indicative of its role in the Golgi​​.
