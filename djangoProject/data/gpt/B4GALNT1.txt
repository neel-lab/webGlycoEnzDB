### Function
Human B4GALNT1 (Beta-1,4-N-acetylgalactosaminyltransferase 1) is an enzyme involved in the biosynthesis of complex gangliosides. It catalyzes the transfer of N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc) from UDP-GalNAc to ganglioside GM3, producing GM2. This enzyme is crucial for the synthesis of gangliosides, which are essential components of neuronal cell membranes and play significant roles in cell-cell communication, neurodevelopment, and signal transduction.

B4GALNT1 facilitates the transfer of GalNAc to GM3, resulting in the formation of GM2. The pathway progresses with the conversion of GM2 to GM1 and other complex gangliosides. These gangliosides are vital for maintaining the structure and function of neuronal cells, contributing to processes such as synaptic transmission and cell recognition.

B4GALNT1 is localized in the Golgi apparatus, where it functions as a type II transmembrane protein. The Golgi apparatus is the central hub for protein and lipid glycosylation, ensuring that B4GALNT1 can effectively contribute to the synthesis of complex gangliosides necessary for proper cellular function.

### Diseases
Mutations in the B4GALNT1 gene are associated with hereditary spastic paraplegia subtype 26 (SPG26), a progressive motor neuropathy. Patients with SPG26 experience symptoms such as spasticity, muscle weakness, and progressive difficulty in walking. The absence of complex gangliosides due to B4GALNT1 mutations disrupts neuronal function and integrity, leading to neurodegeneration and the clinical manifestations of SPG26. Research on knockout mice models has shown similar symptoms, further underscoring the enzyme's critical role in maintaining neuronal health.
