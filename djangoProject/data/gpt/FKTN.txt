### Function

Human FKTN (fukutin) is an essential glycosyltransferase enzyme involved in the post-translational modification of alpha-dystroglycan (α-DG), a glycoprotein critical for the structural integrity of muscle tissue. FKTN specifically facilitates the addition of a unique phosphorylated O-mannosyl glycan to α-DG. This modification is crucial for the binding of α-DG to extracellular matrix proteins like laminin, which stabilizes muscle cells and maintains the connection between the cytoskeleton and the extracellular matrix .

### Reaction Pathways

FKTN functions in the complex O-mannosyl glycosylation pathway, where it catalyzes the transfer of sugar molecules to α-DG after an initial phosphorylation step. This enzymatic process is vital for the proper glycosylation of α-DG, which enables it to form a functional receptor for extracellular matrix components. The pathway involves several enzymes, with FKTN playing a central role in the addition of phosphorylated sugar residues that are necessary for the structural and functional integrity of glycoproteins  .

### Location

FKTN is localized in the Golgi apparatus, where it participates in the glycosylation of proteins destined for the cell membrane or extracellular space. The Golgi is the primary site for protein processing and modification, allowing FKTN to interact efficiently with other glycosylation enzymes to ensure that α-DG is properly modified before being transported to its functional site on the cell surface .

### Diseases

Mutations in the FKTN gene are associated with Fukuyama congenital muscular dystrophy (FCMD), a severe form of muscular dystrophy characterized by muscle weakness, abnormal brain development, and structural defects in the eyes. FCMD is particularly prevalent in Japan and is caused by a retrotransposon insertion in the FKTN gene, leading to reduced enzyme function. The disease highlights the critical role of FKTN in muscle integrity and neurodevelopment, as the improper glycosylation of α-DG due to FKTN mutations disrupts muscle cell stability and neuronal migration  .

In summary, FKTN is a crucial enzyme for the glycosylation of α-DG, ensuring the stability and function of muscle tissues. Its location in the Golgi apparatus allows it to perform its essential role in protein modification, with mutations leading to severe congenital muscular dystrophies.
