### Function
Human RPN1, or ribophorin I, is an essential component of the oligosaccharyltransferase (OST) complex, which plays a critical role in the N-linked glycosylation of proteins. This process involves the attachment of a sugar moiety to asparagine residues on nascent proteins, a modification crucial for protein folding, stability, and function. RPN1 serves as a receptor for the OST complex, anchoring it to the rough endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane and facilitating the transfer of oligosaccharides to newly synthesized proteins.

### Reaction Pathways
RPN1 functions within the N-linked glycosylation pathway, a key post-translational modification process. In this pathway, the OST complex, with RPN1 as a core subunit, catalyzes the transfer of an oligosaccharide precursor from a lipid carrier (dolichol) to the asparagine residues of nascent polypeptides as they are translocated into the ER. This glycosylation is crucial for the proper folding and quality control of proteins before they proceed to the Golgi apparatus for further processing and sorting.

### Location
RPN1 is localized in the membrane of the rough endoplasmic reticulum (ER), where it anchors the OST complex. The ER is the site of protein synthesis and initial post-translational modifications, making it the ideal location for RPN1 to facilitate N-linked glycosylation. The rough ER's proximity to ribosomes allows RPN1 to interact with nascent polypeptides as they are synthesized, ensuring efficient glycosylation.

### Diseases
Mutations or dysfunction in RPN1 can lead to disorders related to protein misfolding and glycosylation defects.
