### Function
Human HS3ST2, or heparan sulfate 3-O-sulfotransferase 2, is an enzyme that plays a crucial role in modifying heparan sulfate, a glycosaminoglycan involved in various biological processes. HS3ST2 catalyzes the transfer of a sulfate group to the 3-O position of glucosamine residues within the heparan sulfate chains. This specific sulfation is important for creating binding sites for a variety of proteins, influencing processes such as cell signaling, blood coagulation, and neuronal development.

The specific 3-O-sulfation by HS3ST2 is particularly significant in modulating signaling pathways that are critical for cellular communication and development.
