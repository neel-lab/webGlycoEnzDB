### Function

Human CHST7 (Carbohydrate Sulfotransferase 7), also known as chondroitin 6-O-sulfotransferase-2 (C6ST-2), is an enzyme that catalyzes the transfer of sulfate groups from the donor molecule 3'-phosphoadenosine-5'-phosphosulfate (PAPS) to the 6-position of N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc) residues in chondroitin. This sulfation process is crucial for the formation of chondroitin 6-sulfate, which plays a vital role in maintaining the structural integrity and function of the extracellular matrix.
