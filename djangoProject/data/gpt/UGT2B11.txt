### Function
Human UGT2B11, or UDP-glucuronosyltransferase 2B11, is an enzyme involved in the glucuronidation of various endogenous and exogenous compounds. UGT2B11 plays a key role in the detoxification process by catalyzing the conjugation of glucuronic acid to substances like steroids, bile acids, and certain medications. This glucuronidation process enhances the solubility of these compounds, making them more easily excreted from the body, thereby helping maintain metabolic balance and protecting against potentially harmful substances.

### Reaction Pathways
UGT2B11 operates within the glucuronidation pathway, a crucial phase II metabolic process. The enzyme transfers glucuronic acid from the donor molecule UDP-glucuronic acid to hydroxyl, carboxyl, or amino groups on its substrates. UGT2B11 is particularly involved in the metabolism of androgens, estrogens, and other steroid hormones, as well as xenobiotics, including some pharmaceutical drugs. This enzymatic activity is essential for regulating hormone levels and facilitating the elimination of drugs and toxins from the body.

### Location
UGT2B11 is predominantly expressed in the liver, which is the central organ for detoxification and drug metabolism. The liver's role in filtering blood and processing chemicals makes it an ideal site for UGT2B11's activity. The enzyme may also be expressed in other tissues, such as the kidneys and gastrointestinal tract, where it contributes to the localized metabolism of substances before their excretion.
