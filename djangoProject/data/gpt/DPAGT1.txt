### Function

Human DPAGT1 (dolichyl-phosphate (UDP-N-acetylglucosamine) N-acetylglucosaminephosphotransferase 1) is an essential enzyme in the N-linked glycosylation pathway. It catalyzes the initial step of this pathway, transferring N-acetylglucosamine-1-phosphate (GlcNAc-1-P) from UDP-GlcNAc to dolichol phosphate (Dol-P), forming GlcNAc-P-P-Dol. This reaction is crucial as it initiates the assembly of the oligosaccharide precursor used in N-linked glycosylation, a process vital for proper protein folding and stability.

### Reaction Pathways

DPAGT1 is a key player in the biosynthesis of lipid-linked oligosaccharides (LLOs), which are essential for N-linked glycosylation. The enzyme catalyzes the first step of LLO synthesis in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). This involves the transfer of GlcNAc from UDP-GlcNAc to Dol-P on the cytoplasmic side of the ER membrane, creating GlcNAc-P-P-Dol. This molecule then undergoes further modifications, including the addition of other sugar residues, eventually forming a complete LLO that is transferred to nascent proteins, ensuring their proper glycosylation.

### Location

DPAGT1 is located in the endoplasmic reticulum membrane, where it functions as part of the initial stages of glycoprotein synthesis. The enzyme is embedded in the ER membrane, with its active site facing the cytoplasm to access its substrates, UDP-GlcNAc and Dol-P. This strategic localization allows DPAGT1 to efficiently participate in the N-glycosylation pathway, directly interacting with other enzymes involved in the process.

### Diseases

Mutations in the DPAGT1 gene can lead to significant health issues, primarily congenital disorders of glycosylation (CDG), specifically CDG-Ij. These disorders are characterized by defects in glycoprotein biosynthesis, leading to severe developmental and metabolic abnormalities. Symptoms of DPAGT1-related CDG include muscular hypotonia, developmental delays, intellectual disability, and, in severe cases, early childhood death.
