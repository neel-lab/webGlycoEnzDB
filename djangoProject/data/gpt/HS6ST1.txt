### Function
Human HS6ST1, or heparan sulfate 6-O-sulfotransferase 1, is an enzyme that plays a key role in modifying heparan sulfate, a glycosaminoglycan involved in various biological processes. HS6ST1 specifically catalyzes the transfer of a sulfate group to the 6-O position of glucosamine residues within heparan sulfate chains. This sulfation is crucial for regulating the interaction of heparan sulfate with a range of proteins, influencing processes such as cell signaling, development, and blood coagulation.
