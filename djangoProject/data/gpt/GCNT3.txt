### Function
Human GCNT3, or glucosaminyl (N-acetyl) transferase 3, is an enzyme that plays a crucial role in the biosynthesis of branched O-glycans. GCNT3 specifically catalyzes the addition of N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) to core 1 O-glycan structures, leading to the formation of core 2 O-glycans. These branched glycan structures are essential for various cellular processes, including mucin production, cell signaling, and immune responses.

The core 2 O-glycans synthesized by GCNT3 are important in maintaining the structure and function of mucins, as well as in regulating cell-cell interactions and immune responses.

GCNT3 is primarily localized in the Golgi apparatus, the organelle responsible for protein processing and modification. ### Diseases
Alterations in GCNT3 activity have been associated with various diseases, particularly in the context of cancer and inflammatory conditions. Overexpression of GCNT3 has been linked to certain cancers, such as colorectal and pancreatic cancers, where it contributes to altered glycosylation patterns that can enhance tumor cell adhesion, migration, and invasion. Additionally, GCNT3 is involved in the regulation of mucin production, and dysregulation of this enzyme has been implicated in conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), where excessive mucus production is a hallmark.
