Human Alpha-1,4-N-acetylglucosaminyltransferase (A4GNT) is a glycosyltransferase enzyme primarily involved in the biosynthesis of mucin-type O-glycans. This enzyme catalyzes the transfer of N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) in an alpha-1,4 linkage to core 2 branched O-glycans, a process critical in the formation of complex and branched structures of mucins.

A4GNT is predominantly expressed in stomach and pancreas tissues. Immunohistochemical studies have shown that A4GNT protein is localized in the Golgi apparatus of mucous cells secreting gland mucin in the gastroduodenal mucosa. It is also expressed in pancreatic ducts showing gastric metaplasia, indicating a specific role in gastrointestinal tissue functions​​.

The significance of A4GNT in human health is highlighted by its association with gastrointestinal diseases. For example, the activity of A4GNT has been linked to Helicobacter pylori-related diseases, such as chronic active gastritis, gastric adenocarcinoma, and gastric mucosa-associated lymphoid tissue lymphoma. Studies suggest that the growth and motility of H. pylori can be affected by the presence of mucins synthesized with the involvement of A4GNT, indicating a potential role in the pathogenesis or progression of these diseases​​.

Additionally, loss of A4GNT activity has been implicated in tumor-promoting inflammation. Knockout mice deficient in A4GNT have shown increased inflammatory cell infiltration and angiogenesis in the gastric mucosa, suggesting that the absence of A4GNT-mediated glycosylation could contribute to gastric carcinogenesis​​.
