### Function
Human SLC2A1, also known as glucose transporter type 1 (GLUT1), is a critical protein that facilitates the transport of glucose across cell membranes. Glucose is a primary energy source for cells, and SLC2A1 ensures that glucose can enter cells from the bloodstream, enabling them to produce energy through glycolysis and other metabolic pathways. GLUT1 is especially important in tissues with high glucose demands, such as the brain, red blood cells, and the placenta.

### Reaction Pathways
SLC2A1 functions in the glucose transport pathway. It operates as a facilitative transporter, moving glucose down its concentration gradient from areas of higher concentration (like the bloodstream) into cells. GLUT1 does not require energy (ATP) to function, as it relies on the passive diffusion of glucose. This process is essential for maintaining cellular energy levels, particularly in cells that rely heavily on glucose as their primary fuel source.

### Location
SLC2A1 is widely expressed in various tissues, with high levels in the brain, red blood cells, endothelial cells of blood-brain barrier, and the placenta. Its localization in the plasma membrane of these cells allows it to effectively regulate glucose uptake, ensuring that these tissues receive a constant supply of glucose necessary for their energy needs. In the brain, GLUT1 is critical for transporting glucose across the blood-brain barrier, providing the primary energy source for neuronal function.

### Diseases
Mutations in the SLC2A1 gene are associated with a condition known as GLUT1 deficiency syndrome (GLUT1-DS). This rare genetic disorder is characterized by insufficient glucose transport into the brain, leading to a range of neurological symptoms, including seizures, developmental delays, motor problems, and cognitive impairments. Since glucose is the primary energy source for the brain, the deficiency severely impacts brain function. GLUT1 deficiency syndrome is typically treated with a ketogenic diet, which provides an alternative energy source (ketones) to the brain.
