### Function

Human FUT5 (fucosyltransferase 5) is an enzyme involved in the biosynthesis of complex fucosylated glycan structures. It catalyzes the transfer of fucose from GDP-fucose to the N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) residues on glycoproteins and glycolipids. FUT5 is particularly known for its role in creating the sialyl Lewis x (sLex) epitope, which is important in cell-cell adhesion, particularly in the interaction between leukocytes and endothelial cells during inflammation.

### Reaction Pathways

FUT5 functions within the broader fucosylation pathway, where it adds fucose in an α1,3 or α1,4 linkage to GlcNAc residues. This enzymatic activity is a key step in the formation of sLex, a glycan structure that plays a critical role in mediating leukocyte adhesion to endothelial cells via selectin binding. ### Location

FUT5 is localized in the Golgi apparatus of cells, where it participates in the final stages of glycoprotein and glycolipid synthesis. The Golgi is the cellular hub for protein modification and sorting, making it the ideal environment for FUT5 to carry out its glycosyltransferase functions. This localization ensures that the fucosylation of glycan structures occurs properly before they are transported to the cell surface or secreted.

### Diseases

While specific diseases directly caused by FUT5 mutations have not been well-documented, alterations in FUT5 expression and activity have been implicated in various pathological conditions, including cancer and inflammatory diseases. For instance, the overexpression of sLex, a product of FUT5 activity, has been associated with the increased metastatic potential of certain cancers due to its role in cell adhesion and migration. Additionally, FUT5 activity may be modulated during viral infections, further linking it to disease processes.
