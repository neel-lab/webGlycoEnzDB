Human POMT1 (Protein O-Mannosyltransferase 1) is an essential enzyme in the biosynthesis of O-mannosyl glycan, a type of glycosylation crucial for the proper functioning of certain proteins, particularly dystroglycan. POMT1, often working in conjunction with its homolog POMT2, is involved in transferring mannose to serine or threonine residues in the dystroglycan protein.

Located in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), POMT1's activity is vital for the early steps of O-mannosylation, a process necessary for the proper maturation and function of alpha-dystroglycan (α-DG). α-DG is a key component of the dystrophin-glycoprotein complex, critical for maintaining the structural integrity of muscle fibers and for the interaction between cells and the extracellular matrix.

Mutations in the POMT1 gene can lead to a spectrum of muscular dystrophies known as dystroglycanopathies. These disorders are characterized by a wide range of symptoms, from mild to severe, including muscle weakness, intellectual disability, and abnormalities in brain development. One of the most severe forms of dystroglycanopathy associated with POMT1 mutations is Walker-Warburg Syndrome (WWS), a condition marked by congenital muscular dystrophy, brain malformations, and ocular abnormalities.
