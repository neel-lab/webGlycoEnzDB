### Function
Human POGLUT2, or protein O-glucosyltransferase 2, is an enzyme responsible for adding glucose molecules to specific serine residues within the epidermal growth factor-like (EGF) repeats of proteins, including those involved in the Notch signaling pathway. This process, known as O-glucosylation, is essential for the proper folding, stability, and function of these proteins. While similar to POGLUT1, POGLUT2 targets a different set of substrates, contributing to the diversity of glycosylation patterns in cellular processes.

POGLUT2 operates within the glycosylation pathway, where it catalyzes the transfer of glucose from UDP-glucose (the donor molecule) to the serine residues within EGF-like repeats of target proteins. This modification is crucial for the correct folding and maturation of these proteins, ensuring their functionality in signaling pathways such as Notch, which regulates cell differentiation, proliferation, and apoptosis. The activity of POGLUT2 is important for maintaining the integrity and activity of these glycoproteins.

### Location
POGLUT2 is localized in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), where it participates in the early stages of protein synthesis and modification. The ER is the site where newly synthesized proteins are folded and undergo initial post-translational modifications, including O-glucosylation by POGLUT2. This localization is critical for ensuring that proteins are correctly modified before being transported to the Golgi apparatus for further processing and eventual deployment to the cell membrane or other cellular locations.
