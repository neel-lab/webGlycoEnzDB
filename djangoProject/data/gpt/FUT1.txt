### Function

Human FUT1 (fucosyltransferase 1) is an enzyme crucial for the biosynthesis of the H antigen, a key component in the ABO blood group system. FUT1 catalyzes the transfer of fucose from GDP-fucose to the terminal galactose of oligosaccharide chains on glycoproteins and glycolipids, forming the H antigen. This antigen serves as the precursor for A and B antigens, depending on the subsequent actions of A and B transferases. FUT1's activity is therefore essential for determining the blood type of an individual.

### Reaction Pathways

FUT1 is involved in the fucosylation pathway, where it transfers an alpha-1,2-linked fucose residue to the terminal galactose of Type 1 or Type 2 oligosaccharides. This step is necessary to produce the H antigen, which is further modified by glycosyltransferases specific to the A or B blood group antigens. The reaction catalyzed by FUT1 is the final step in creating the substrate necessary for blood group antigen expression, linking this enzyme directly to the blood group determination pathway.

### Location

FUT1 is primarily expressed in erythrocyte precursors, endothelial cells, and epithelial tissues, where it synthesizes the H antigen on the surface of red blood cells and other cells. The enzyme is localized to the Golgi apparatus, where glycosylation reactions, including fucosylation, take place. This Golgi localization is essential for the proper assembly and processing of glycan chains before they are transported to the cell surface.

### Diseases

Mutations in the FUT1 gene can lead to rare conditions such as the Bombay phenotype, where individuals lack the H antigen on their red blood cells. This results in a blood type that does not express A, B, or H antigens, leading to severe transfusion complications since these individuals can only receive blood from other Bombay phenotype donors. Additionally, altered expression or mutations in FUT1 have been linked to certain types of cancer, as fucosylation patterns are often disrupted in tumor cells, affecting cell adhesion, migration, and immune evasion.

In summary, FUT1 is a key enzyme in the fucosylation pathway, essential for the synthesis of the H antigen and thus for determining blood type. It is localized in the Golgi apparatus and plays a critical role in normal physiology and disease.
