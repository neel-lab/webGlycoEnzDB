### Function
Human GALNT11, or polypeptide N-acetylgalactosaminyltransferase 11, is an enzyme that plays a critical role in the initiation of O-linked glycosylation. GALNT11 catalyzes the transfer of N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc) from UDP-GalNAc to the serine or threonine residues of target proteins, forming the core O-GalNAc glycan structure. This glycosylation is crucial for modifying proteins, affecting their stability, function, and interactions in various biological processes such as cell signaling and mucin production.

### Reaction Pathways
GALNT11 is involved in the O-glycosylation pathway, specifically initiating the synthesis of mucin-type O-GalNAc glycans. By transferring GalNAc to serine or threonine residues, GALNT11 creates the foundational structure upon which additional sugars can be added by other glycosyltransferases. This step is essential for the creation of complex glycan chains that contribute to the proper functioning of glycoproteins, particularly in tissues that produce mucus, such as the respiratory and digestive tracts.

### Location
GALNT11 is primarily localized in the Golgi apparatus, the cellular organelle responsible for processing and modifying proteins. Within the Golgi, GALNT11 participates in the glycosylation of newly synthesized proteins, ensuring they receive the necessary glycan modifications before being transported to their final destinations. This Golgi localization is critical for GALNT11 to access and modify its target proteins during their maturation process.

### Diseases
Mutations or dysregulation of GALNT11 have been implicated in several diseases. Notably, GALNT11 has been linked to congenital heart defects, particularly heterotaxy, a condition where the internal organs are abnormally arranged within the body. Research suggests that GALNT11 influences the development of cilia, cellular structures important for determining left-right asymmetry in the body. Abnormal GALNT11 function can lead to defects in ciliary function, contributing to disorders like heterotaxy. Additionally, GALNT11 dysregulation may play a role in certain cancers by altering glycosylation patterns on proteins involved in tumor growth and metastasis.
