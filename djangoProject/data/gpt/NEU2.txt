### Function
Human NEU2, or neuraminidase 2, is an enzyme that plays a critical role in the catabolism of sialic acids, which are important components of glycoproteins and glycolipids. NEU2 specifically hydrolyzes terminal sialic acid residues from these sialylated molecules, aiding in their degradation and recycling. This function is essential for maintaining cellular homeostasis and regulating processes like cell signaling, immune response, and cell-cell interactions.

### Reaction Pathways

The removal of sialic acids by NEU2 is a critical step that influences the composition of cell surfaces and the function of various biomolecules, impacting cellular interactions and signaling pathways.

This distinct localization allows NEU2 to function in a different cellular environment compared to lysosomal neuraminidases like NEU1. The cytoplasmic presence of NEU2 suggests its involvement in the degradation of sialylated molecules within the cytoplasm or those that are recycled from the cell surface.
