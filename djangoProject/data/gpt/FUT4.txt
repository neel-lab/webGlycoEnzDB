### Function

Human FUT4 (fucosyltransferase 4) is an enzyme involved in the synthesis of Lewis antigens, specifically the Lewis x (Lex) antigen. FUT4 transfers a fucose residue from GDP-fucose to N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) residues in an Î±1,3 linkage on glycoproteins and glycolipids. This fucosylation is crucial for the formation of Lex structures, which play a significant role in cell adhesion, migration, and immune response, particularly in leukocyte trafficking during inflammation .

### Reaction Pathways

FUT4 functions within the fucosylation pathway, where it catalyzes the addition of fucose to Type 2 oligosaccharides, forming Lex structures. These structures are essential ligands for selectins, which are cell adhesion molecules that mediate the interaction between leukocytes and the endothelium. This process is vital for leukocyte rolling and extravasation during immune responses. FUT4, along with other fucosyltransferases like FUT7, contributes to the synthesis of these important glycans .

### Location

FUT4 is primarily localized in the Golgi apparatus of cells, where it carries out its glycosyltransferase activity. The Golgi provides the necessary environment for FUT4 to interact with other enzymes and substrates involved in glycan synthesis. The enzyme's location in the Golgi is crucial for the proper processing and maturation of glycoproteins and glycolipids before they are transported to the cell surface or secreted .

### Diseases

Altered expression or activity of FUT4 has been implicated in several diseases, particularly in cancer and inflammatory conditions. Overexpression of FUT4 is often observed in various cancers, where it contributes to increased tumor cell invasiveness and metastasis by enhancing cell adhesion and migration through the upregulation of Lex antigens. Additionally, FUT4 plays a role in inflammatory diseases due to its involvement in leukocyte trafficking, making it a potential target for therapeutic intervention in conditions characterized by excessive inflammation .

In summary, FUT4 is a critical enzyme in the biosynthesis of Lex antigens, playing a key role in immune response and cancer progression. Its Golgi localization ensures the proper fucosylation of glycoproteins and glycolipids, which is essential for normal cellular function and disease development.
