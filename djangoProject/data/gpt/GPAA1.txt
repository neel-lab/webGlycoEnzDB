Human GPAA1, or Glycosylphosphatidylinositol Anchor Attachment 1, is a crucial enzyme in the biosynthesis of glycosylphosphatidylinositol (GPI) anchors.

Function
GPAA1 functions as a metallo-protease/synthase and is predicted to catalyze the formation of the amide bond between the GPI anchor and proteins. GPI anchors are glycolipid structures that tether proteins to the cell membrane. The role of GPAA1 is pivotal in attaching these anchors to specific proteins, a process essential for the proper localization and function of these GPI-anchored proteins (GPI-APs).

Reaction Pathways
GPAA1 participates in the GPI anchor biosynthesis pathway. The enzyme aids in the attachment of GPI anchors to the C-terminus of proteins, a modification that enables these proteins to be anchored to the cell membrane. GPI-anchored proteins play roles in various cellular processes, including signaling, cell adhesion, and immune response.

Location
GPAA1 is located in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), where GPI anchor biosynthesis occurs. The active sites of GPAA1 face the lumen of the ER, facilitating the interaction with protein substrates that require GPI anchoring.

Related Diseases
While specific diseases directly associated with GPAA1 dysfunction are not detailed in the cited material, defects in GPI anchor biosynthesis can lead to a variety of diseases. These include paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria (PNH), a rare acquired hematological disorder, and certain congenital disorders of glycosylation. Abnormal GPI anchoring affects the membrane localization and function of proteins, which can have wide-ranging effects on cell function and health.
