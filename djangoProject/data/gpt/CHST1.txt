CHST1 specifically catalyzes the transfer of sulfate groups to the galactose residues in keratan sulfate. This sulfation is a key post-translational modification that enhances the ability of keratan sulfate to interact with other molecules and to participate in various biological processes. Sulfated keratan sulfate is found in several tissues, including the cornea, cartilage, and the nervous system, where it plays roles in cell signaling, ion exchange, and structural integrity. The sulfation of keratan sulfate by CHST1 is essential for maintaining its charge density and binding properties, which are critical for tissue hydration and resilience.

Location:
CHST1 is located in the Golgi apparatus, where it participates in the sulfation of glycosaminoglycans. The enzyme's presence in the Golgi apparatus is strategic for modifying keratan sulfate as it is synthesized and prepared for transport to its destination in the extracellular matrix or on cell surfaces.

Mutations or dysfunctions in CHST1 can lead to abnormalities in keratan sulfate sulfation, potentially contributing to a range of diseases. One such condition is macular corneal dystrophy, a disorder affecting the cornea and leading to vision impairment. Proper sulfation of keratan sulfate is crucial for corneal clarity and function. Additionally, aberrations in CHST1 activity could impact joint and skeletal health due to its role in cartilage structure and function.
