### Function

Human DPY19L1 (dpy-19-like 1) is a crucial enzyme involved in the biosynthesis of dolichol-phosphate mannose (Dol-P-Man), a key intermediate in various glycosylation pathways. The enzyme catalyzes the transfer of mannose from GDP-mannose to dolichol-phosphate, forming Dol-P-Man. This product is essential for the proper glycosylation of proteins and lipids, ensuring their stability, function, and localization within the cell. Glycosylation is critical for numerous cellular processes, including cell signaling, adhesion, and immune response.

### Reaction Pathways

DPY19L1 is involved in the dolichol-linked oligosaccharide pathway, which is a critical part of the N-linked glycosylation process. The enzyme operates by transferring mannose from GDP-mannose to dolichol-phosphate in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane. This reaction forms Dol-P-Man, which then participates in the synthesis of glycoproteins. Dol-P-Man acts as a mannose donor in several glycosylation reactions, including the formation of glycosylphosphatidylinositol (GPI) anchors and O-mannosylation, essential for the function of various proteins.

DPY19L1 is localized in the endoplasmic reticulum membrane, where it forms part of the dolichol-phosphate mannose synthase complex. This localization is strategic, allowing the enzyme to directly interact with other components involved in glycosylation processes. The ER is the primary site for the synthesis and initial post-translational modification of proteins, making the presence of DPY19L1 crucial for efficient glycosylation and protein processing.

These disorders are characterized by defects in glycoprotein biosynthesis, resulting in a range of symptoms, including developmental delays, intellectual disabilities, hypotonia, and various other systemic issues. Additionally, improper glycosylation due to DPY19L1 mutations can affect the function and stability of glycoproteins and glycolipids, leading to broader metabolic and physiological disturbances.
