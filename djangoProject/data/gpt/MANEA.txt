Human MANEA, or mannosidase endo-alpha, is an enzyme involved in the processing of glycoproteins, specifically in the trimming of glycan structures. MANEA specifically catalyzes the hydrolysis of mannose-rich oligosaccharides, targeting the alpha-mannosidic linkages in glycoproteins. This enzymatic activity is essential for the proper maturation and function of glycoproteins, which play critical roles in cellular communication, protein folding, and immune responses.

MANEA’s activity is a key step in ensuring that glycoproteins achieve the correct structure needed for their roles in various biological processes.
