### Function
Human MAN1A2, or alpha-1,2-mannosidase 1A2, is an enzyme that plays a key role in the N-glycosylation pathway, which is essential for the proper folding, quality control, and function of glycoproteins. MAN1A2 specifically trims alpha-1,2-linked mannose residues from high-mannose N-glycans on newly synthesized proteins. This trimming is a crucial step in converting these high-mannose structures into complex or hybrid N-glycans, which are necessary for various cellular processes.

These modifications are critical for protein stability, localization, and interaction with other cellular molecules.

CDGs are a group of inherited metabolic disorders that result from defects in the glycosylation process, leading to a wide range of symptoms, including developmental delays, neurological issues, and immune deficiencies. Additionally, alterations in glycosylation patterns, which could involve enzymes like MAN1A2, have been linked to cancer, where they may affect tumor progression, metastasis, and immune evasion.
