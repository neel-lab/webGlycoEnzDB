### Function
Human GXYLT1, or glucoside xylosyltransferase 1, is an enzyme involved in the post-translational modification of proteins, specifically in the glycosylation process that leads to the formation of O-linked glycosaminoglycans (GAGs). These GAG chains are essential for various cellular functions, including cell signaling, adhesion, and structural integrity.
