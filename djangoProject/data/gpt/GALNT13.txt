### Function
Human GALNT13, or polypeptide N-acetylgalactosaminyltransferase 13, is an enzyme that plays a key role in the O-linked glycosylation process. GALNT13 transfers N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc) from UDP-GalNAc to the serine or threonine residues on specific proteins, initiating the formation of O-GalNAc glycan structures. This modification is essential for the proper function, stability, and interaction of glycoproteins involved in various biological processes, including cell signaling and adhesion.

This localization is critical for GALNT13 to access and modify its target proteins efficiently.
