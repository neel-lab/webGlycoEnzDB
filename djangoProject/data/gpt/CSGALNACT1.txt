### Function

Human CSGALNACT1 (Chondroitin Sulfate N-Acetylgalactosaminyltransferase 1) is an enzyme crucial for the biosynthesis of chondroitin sulfate, a major component of the extracellular matrix. It catalyzes the transfer of N-acetylgalactosamine (GalNAc) from UDP-GalNAc to the glucuronic acid residues on the growing chondroitin sulfate chains. CSGALNACT1 is localized in the Golgi apparatus, where it functions as a type II transmembrane protein. The localization within the Golgi is essential for its role in the post-translational modification of proteoglycans, ensuring that chondroitin sulfate chains are properly synthesized and elongated before being transported to their final destinations in the extracellular matrix.
