Human GNPDA1, or Glucosamine-6-Phosphate Deaminase 1, is an enzyme involved in the hexosamine biosynthesis pathway.

Function
GNPDA1 catalyzes the deamination of glucosamine-6-phosphate to fructose-6-phosphate and ammonia, playing a critical role in the regulation of the hexosamine biosynthesis pathway. This pathway is important for producing UDP-N-acetylglucosamine (UDP-GlcNAc), a substrate used in the glycosylation of proteins and lipids. GNPDA1 helps regulate the flow of glucose into the hexosamine pathway, balancing between the needs for energy production (glycolysis) and glycosylation.

Reaction Pathways
GNPDA1 is involved in the hexosamine biosynthesis pathway, which integrates glucose metabolism with the production of glycosylated end products. The pathway starts with the conversion of fructose-6-phosphate to glucosamine-6-phosphate, which GNPDA1 can then deaminate, effectively modulating the amount of substrate available for the production of UDP-GlcNAc. This regulation is crucial as UDP-GlcNAc is used in the glycosylation of proteins and lipids, affecting various cellular functions.
